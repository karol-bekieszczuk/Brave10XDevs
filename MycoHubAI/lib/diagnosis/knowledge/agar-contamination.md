# Agar contamination signals

## Fast spreading non-mycelial growth

Watch for growth that expands much faster than the intended culture, appears in isolated patches away from the inoculation point, or changes color over time. Green, black, pink, or intensely colored patches are stronger contamination signals than slow, uniform white growth.

Treat the observation as uncertain when the log only says that the plate "looks odd" or "changed" without location, timing, color, or texture. In that case, ask for direct visual observations from the same selected plate before suggesting a cause.

## Wet or bacterial-looking areas

Glossy wet patches, pooling liquid, slimy texture, or growth that looks translucent rather than filamentous can indicate bacterial competition or excess moisture. Condensation alone is not enough to diagnose contamination; compare it with growth shape, spread pattern, and whether healthy mycelium is advancing.

Keep actions conservative: isolate the plate from clean work, avoid opening it unnecessarily, record what changed, and transfer only from clearly healthy leading edges when the user already has a clean transfer workflow.
