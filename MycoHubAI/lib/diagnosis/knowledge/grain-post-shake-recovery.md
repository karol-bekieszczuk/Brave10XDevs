# Grain post-shake recovery

## Expected recovery window

After shaking grain, visible recovery can lag before mycelium reconnects across kernels. A short pause immediately after a shake is not automatically a failure. The selected log should anchor the answer in the shake date, percent colonized before shake, and whether new white growth appears after 48 hours.

If the user asks before enough time has passed, return a low-confidence or missing-context answer and ask for a narrow follow-up about direct visual changes since the shake.

## Concerning post-shake patterns

Concerning signs include no visible recovery after more than 48 hours, wet clumping, spreading discoloration, or growth that was strong before the shake but stops afterward. These patterns may suggest stressed culture, bacterial competition, overly wet grain, or poor distribution.

Suggested actions should remain text-log based: keep the container closed, note daily recovery, avoid repeated shaking without a clear reason, and compare new growth against the selected log's pre-shake condition.
