# Agar healthy and slow growth patterns

## Healthy early growth

Healthy agar growth is usually organized around the inoculation point or transfer wedge and tends to expand with a consistent leading edge. White, filamentous, or rhizomorphic texture can be normal, but color and texture must be interpreted with timing and plate conditions from the selected log.

When the selected log gives only a short time window, avoid over-diagnosing. Early plates may show little visible change before growth becomes obvious.

## Slow or stalled agar growth

Slow agar growth can come from cool incubation temperature, old or stressed culture material, overly wet or overly dry media, weak transfer material, or competition from subtle contamination. The selected log should mention timing, inoculation date, temperature range, visible edge behavior, and whether growth has stopped or is just slow.

Suggested actions should focus on observation and containment: continue tracking daily changes, compare the leading edge over time, keep the plate closed, and consider a new transfer from the healthiest visible edge if the culture is still advancing.
