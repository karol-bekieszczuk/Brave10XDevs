# Grain moisture and stall signs

## Excess moisture indicators

Very wet kernels, pooling liquid, burst grains, sticky clumps, or dense uncolonized areas can point toward excess moisture or bacterial competition. These signs are stronger when growth stalls for several days and the selected log reports that colonized areas stop expanding.

Do not treat moisture alone as a certain cause. The answer should explain uncertainty and connect possible causes to the selected log's timeline, shake history, and visible grain condition.

## Dry or uneven grain behavior

Dry-looking kernels, sparse growth, or colonization only near the inoculation point may indicate low moisture, weak inoculation, poor distribution, or incubation conditions outside the culture's preferred range. Uneven colonization can also happen before a planned shake, so the timeline matters.

Conservative actions include continuing observation, checking whether growth is expanding between log entries, avoiding unnecessary disturbance, and comparing colonized versus uncolonized areas in the same jar or bag.
